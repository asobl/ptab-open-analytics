Great question. NPEs (Non-Practicing Entities) are companies that hold patents but don't actually make anything. They buy up patents and use them to sue operating companies. Sometimes called patent trolls.

The top of the data is dominated by them because of how IPRs work. When an NPE sues 50 companies with the same patent, all 50 defendants can each file their own IPR challenge to kill that patent. One assertion campaign generates dozens of cases, so NPEs show up concentrated at the top of any volume ranking.

But if you look at the long tail of the data, the thousands of entities that show up once or twice, that's almost certainly operating companies and startups getting challenged by a specific competitor who views their patents as a threat.

The 60% institution rate applies to everyone equally. A startup with 3 patents facing their first IPR has the same odds as Apple. They just have a lot less firepower to fight back.
